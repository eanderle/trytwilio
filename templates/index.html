    Welcome!


    With TryTwilio, you'll learn how to harness the power of your telephone by writing code in a language called TwiML. Never written code or worked with TwiML before? Don't worry; we'll take you step-by-step.

    What is Twilio?

    Twilio is a service that empowers you to include voice and messaging functionality into your web and mobile applications.

    What is TwiML?

    TwiML is a set of verbs created by Twilio that allows you to control what happens when you receive an incoming call or text message (SMS). Using TwiML, you can do really neat things such as record a call, play a message for your caller, prompt your caller to press digits on their keypad, and much, much more.

    Lesson 1: <Response>


    Everything starts with the <Response> verb. It tells Twilio that we're sending back TwiML that will tell Twilio what to do with our incoming call or message.
